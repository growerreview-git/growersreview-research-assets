# Skipped annual vent and fan servicing leading to premature motor failure and crop loss — Diagnostic Flow

Full GrowersReview guide: [Skipped Greenhouse Vent & Fan Servicing: What Growers Miss Before Motor Failure](https://growersreview.com/how-to-diagnose-and-prevent-skipped-vent-and-fan-servicing-leading-to-premature-motor-fail/)

## Step 1 — Identify Visible Symptoms

- Increased energy bills
- System breaks down regularly
- Inadequate airflow for environmental control
- Unusual noises from fan motors

## Step 2 — Verify Root Causes

- Dust and debris accumulation on fan blades and motor windings
- Lack of lubrication on motor bearings
- Loose electrical connections leading to arcing and overheating
- Worn belts causing reduced efficiency and motor strain
- Motor winding resistance for insulation integrity
- Bearing play and noise
- Fan blade balance and cleanliness
- Current draw (amperage) of the motor

## Step 3 — Evaluate Solution Path

- Motors with sealed bearings for high-humidity, dusty greenhouse environments
- Easy access panels for routine cleaning of fan blades and vent louvers
- Corrosion-resistant materials (e.g., stainless steel, marine-grade aluminum) for fan housings and vent components
- Integrated thermal overload protection for fan motors to prevent burnout
